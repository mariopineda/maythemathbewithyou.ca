Order of angles reflect one possible order in which they can be determined.
  • Determine $b$: $b=48^{\circ}$ (Alternate interior angles)
  • Determine $a$: $180-95-a-b=37^{\circ}$ (Straight line angles)
  • Determine $j$: $j=48^{\circ}$ (Alternate interior angles)
  • Determine $d$: $b+48+d=180 \Rightarrow d=84^{\circ}$ (Trignle Sume Theorem)
  • Determine $f$: $f=d=84^{\circ}$ (Opposite angles)
  • Determine $g$: $g+d=180 \Rightarrow g=96^{\circ}$ (Straight line angles)
  • Determine $e$: $e=6=96^{\circ}$ (Opposite angles)
  • Determine $k$: $a+g+k=180 \Rightarrow k=47^{\circ}$ (Triangle Sum Theorem)
  • Determine $h$: $48+h+85=180 \Rightarrow h=47^{\circ}$ (Straight line angles)
  • Determine $i$: $i+e+h=180 \Rightarrow i=37^{\circ}$ (Triangle Sum Theorem)
