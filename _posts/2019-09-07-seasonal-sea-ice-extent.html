The amount of sea ice in the arctic varies seasonally and for the year 2018 it can be modelled by the cubic function $A(m) = 0.069x^3 - 1.3x^2 + 6.04x + 6.73$ where $m$ represents the month (January is month 1 and December is month 12) and $A$ represents the area of the sea ice in millions of square kilometers.

    Sketch the function describing the extent of sea ice. Label the axes and key points.
  1. Determine the maximum area of sea ice coverage to the nearest thousand of a million square kilometer.
  2. Determine the minimum area of sea ice coverage to the nearest thousand of a million square kilometer.
  3. Determine during which month the the sea ice covers the largest area.
  4. Determine during which month the the sea ice covers the smallest area.
  5. What is an appropriate domain and range for this context?

If $n$ is a whole number, then $2n$ is an even number and $2n+(2n+2)+(2n+4)+(2n+6)+(2n+8)+(2n+10)$ is a sum of six consequtive even numbers. This expression can be simplified to: $12n+30 = 6(2n+5)$. Since we were able to factor out 6 it means the sum must be divisible by 6.
