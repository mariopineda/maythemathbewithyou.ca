According to the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) the increase in the concentration of atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2) as a result of the burning of fossil fuels can be modeled by the function $C(x) = 353(1.006)^{x-1990}$ where $C(x)$ is the concentration of CO2 in earth's atmosphere in parts per million (ppm) and $x$ is the year.

  1. Ice core samples show that, prior to the industrial revoluation, the highest atmospheric CO2 concentration over the last 400000 years was 300 ppm. Following the industrial revolution the concentration of CO2 has risen above this historical maximum. Determine in what year the atmospheric CO2 concentration reached 300 ppm. Round your answer to the nearest year.
  2. Determine how many percent higher the CO2 concentration is today compared to the historical maximum of 300 ppm.
{% if false %}

A.

\[ \begin{align} C(x) & = 353(1.006)^{x-1990} \\ 300 & = 353(1.006)^{x-1990} \\ \displaystyle\frac{300}{353} & = 1.006^{x-1990} \\ x & = \displaystyle\frac{\log (300 \div 353)}{\log 1.006} + 1990 \\ x & = 1962 \end{align} \]

The CO2 emission reached 300 ppm in 1962.

B.

Set $x=2019$, $C(x) = 353(1.006)^{2019-1990}$, $C(x)=395.49\ldots$. Calculate the percent increase, $\left( \displaystyle\frac{395.49\ldots}{300} -1 \right) \times 100 = 32$. The atmospheric CO2 concentration is 32% higher in 2019 than the historical maximum.
