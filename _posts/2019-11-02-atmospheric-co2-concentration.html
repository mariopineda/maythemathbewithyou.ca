According to the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) the concentration of atmiospheric carbon dioxide (CO2) can be modeled by the function $C(x) = 353(1.006)^{x-1990}$ where $C(x)$ is the atmospheric CO2 concentration in parts per million (ppm) and $x$ is the year.

  1. Prior to the industrial revoluation, the highest atmospheric CO2 concentration over the last 400000 years was 300 ppm. Dut to the burning of fossil fuels the concentration has recently risen above this historical maximum. Determine in what year the atmospheric CO2 concentration reached 300 ppm. Round your answer to the nearest year.
  2. Determine how many percent higher the CO2 concentration is today compared to the historical maximum of 300 ppm.
