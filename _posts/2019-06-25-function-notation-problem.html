GDP per capita is a measure of a country's economic output that accounts for its number of people. It divides the country's gross domestic product by its total population. The GDP per capita is often used as a measurement of a country's standard of living. It tells you, on average, how prosperous a country's citizens are. The estimated global cost to humanity from global warming is described by $G(t) = 22588 - 168.05472 \times t^2$ where $t$ is temperature increase in deegrees Celcius (C°) and $G(t)$ is the predicted global GDP per capita.

  1. Sketch the graph of $G(t)$, $x:\{0, 8, 1\}$, $y:\{0, 23000, 1000\}$.
  2. Determine the value $G(6)$. Explain what the result represents in this context.
  3. What is the GDP per capita if the temperature does not increase due to global warming?
  4. What amount of temperature increase will result in the GDP per capita being reduced by half?
