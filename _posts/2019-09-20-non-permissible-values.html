State the restrictions on the variable in each of following rational expressions.

  1. $\displaystyle\frac{2y^2-2}{y}$
  2. $\displaystyle\frac{x}{10x+20}$
  3. $\displaystyle\frac{a+3}{(a+4)(a-5)}$
  4. $\displaystyle\frac{1+16x^2}{1-16x^2}$
  5. $\displaystyle\frac{d}{d^4-81}$
  6. $\displaystyle\frac{x-1}{x^2+1}$
{% if true %}

A.

$$ \begin{array}{rcll} 5^{2x+3} & = & 5^7 & \\ 2x+3 & = & 7 & \mbox{Drop the bases} \\ x & = & 2 & \mbox{Solve for x} \end{array} $$

B.

$$ \begin{array}{rcll} 7^{x-2} & = & 343 & \\ 7^{x-2} & = & 7^3 & \mbox{Common bases} \\ x-2 & = & 3 & \mbox{Drop the bases} \\ x & = & 5 & \mbox{Solve for x} \end{array} $$

C.

$$ \begin{array}{rcll} 3^{5x-1} & = & 81^{3x} & \\ 3^{5x-1} & = & (3^4)^{3x} & \mbox{Common bases} \\ 3^{5x-1} & = & 3^{12x} & \mbox{Power of a Power Law} \\ 5x-1 & = & 12x & \mbox{Drop the bases} \\ x & = & -\displaystyle\frac{1}{7} & \mbox{Solve for x} \end{array} $$

D.

$$ \begin{array}{rcll} 5^{4a+2} & = & 25^a & \\ 5^{4a+2} & = & (5^2)^a & \mbox{Common bases} \\ 5^{4a+2} & = & 5^{2a} & \mbox{Power of a Power Law} \\ 4a+2 & = & 2a & \mbox{Drop the bases} \\ a & = & -\displaystyle\frac{2}{2}=-1 & \mbox{Solve for a} \end{array} $$

E.

$$ \begin{array}{rcll} 10^{10x-2} & = & 1000^{20x-42} & \\ 10^{10x-2} & = & (10^3)^{20x-42} & \mbox{Common bases} \\ 10^{10x-2} & = & 10^{3(20x-42)} & \mbox{Power of a Power Law} \\ 10^{10x-2} & = & 10^{60x-126} & \mbox{Distributive property} \\ 10x-2 & = & 60x-126 & \mbox{Drop the bases} \\ 10x-60x & = & -126+2 & \mbox{Solve for x} \\ -50x & = & -124 & \\ x & = & \displaystyle\frac{-124}{-50}=\displaystyle\frac{62}{50}=2.48 \\ \end{array} $$

F.

$$ \begin{array}{rcll} 8^{3x} & = & 4^{1-x} & \\ (2^3)^{3x} & = & (2^2)^{1-x} & \mbox{Common bases} \\ 2^{9x} & = & 2^{2(1-x)} & \mbox{Power of a Power Law} \\ 2^{9x} & = & 2^{2-2x} & \mbox{Distributive property} \\ 9x & = & 2-2x & \mbox{Drop the bases} \\ x & = & \displaystyle\frac{2}{11} & \mbox{Solve for x} \\ \end{array} $$
