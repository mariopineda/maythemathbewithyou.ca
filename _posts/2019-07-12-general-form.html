Determine the equations of the lines given the following information. Express the equation in both slope y-intercept and general forms.

  1. A line that has an x-intercept at 5 and a y-intercept at 1.
  2. A line that passes through the point $f(1)=5$ and is parallel to $3x-y+5=0$.

A.

Since at the x-intercept $y=0$ and at the y-intercept $x=0$ we have the following two points, $(5,0)$ and $(0,1)$. Using these points we can determine the slope of the line, $m=\frac{\displaystyle 0-1}{\displaystyle 5-0}=\frac{\displaystyle -1}{\displaystyle 5}$. We can now express the equation of the line is slope y-intercept form as $y=-\frac{\displaystyle 1}{\displaystyle 5}x+1$. We can convert the slope y-intercept form to general form as follows, $$ \begin{array}{rcll} y & = & -\frac{\displaystyle 1}{\displaystyle 5}x+1 & \\ -\frac{\displaystyle 1}{\displaystyle 5}x-y+1 & = & 0 & \mbox{Subtract y from both sides} \\ (-5) \times(-\frac{\displaystyle 1}{\displaystyle 5}x-y+1) & = & 0 \times (-5) & \mbox{Multiply both sides by -5 to eliminate negative fraction} \\ x+5y-5) & = & 0 & \mbox{Apply distributive property on the LHS} \end{array} $$

B.

Determine the slopes of the two line segments, $m_{AB}$ and $m_{CD}$. $$ \begin{array}{rcl} m_{AB} & = & \frac{\displaystyle y_B-y_A}{\displaystyle x_B-X_A} \\ m_{AB} & = & \frac{\displaystyle -2-5}{\displaystyle 0-3} \\ m_{AB} & = & \frac{\displaystyle -7}{\displaystyle -3} = \frac{\displaystyle 7}{\displaystyle 3} \\ m_{CD} & = & \frac{\displaystyle y_D-y_C}{\displaystyle x_D-x_C} \\ m_{CD} & = & \frac{\displaystyle 2-3}{\displaystyle 3-5} \\ m_{CD} & = & \frac{\displaystyle -1}{\displaystyle -2} = \frac{\displaystyle 1}{\displaystyle 2} \\ \end{array} $$ The line segements are not parallel because $m_{AB} \neq m_{CD}$ and not perpendicular because $m_{AB}\times m_{CD} \neq -1$.
