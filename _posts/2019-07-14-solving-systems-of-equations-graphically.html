Solve by graphing and check your answer algebraically

  1. $$ \begin{array}{rcl} x+y & = & 5 \\ 3x-y & = & 3 \end{array} $$
  2. $$ \begin{array}{rcl} x+y & = & 7 \\ 3x+4y & = & 24 \end{array} $$

A.

Rewrite the system in slope y-intercept form. $$ \begin{array}{rcl} y & = & -x+5 \\ y & = & 3x-3 \end{array} $$ Graph the two equations and visually determine the coordinates of the point of intersection.

The solution to this system is $(2,3)$. Verify by substituting solution into the original system and evaluate LHS and RHS. $$ \begin{array}{rcl} x+y & = & 5 \\ 2+3 & = & 5 \\ 5 & = & 5 \\ \mbox{LHS} & = & \mbox{RHS} \\ & & \\ 3x-y & = & 3 \\ 3 \times 2 - 3 & = & 3 \\ 3 & = & 3 \\ \mbox{LHS} & = & \mbox{RHS} \end{array} $$

B.

Rewrite the system in slope y-intercept form. $$ \begin{array}{rcl} y & = & -x+7 \\ y & = & -\frac{\displaystyle 3}{\displaystyle 4}x+6 \end{array} $$

Solution: $(4,3)$. Verify by substituting solution into the original system and evaluate LHS and RHS. $$ \begin{array}{rcl} x+y & = & 7 \\ 4+5 & = & 7 \\ 7 & = & 7 \\ \mbox{LHS} & = & \mbox{RHS} \\ & & \\ 3x+4y & = & 24 \\ 3 \times 4+4 \times 3 & = & 24 \\ 24 & = & 24 \\ \mbox{LHS} & = & \mbox{RHS} \end{array} $$
