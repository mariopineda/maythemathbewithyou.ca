  1. Solve the following system of equation by substitution $$ \begin{array}{rcr} x+3y & = & 9 \\ 2x-y & = & 4 \end{array} $$
  2. Solve the following system of equation by elimination $$ \begin{array}{rcr} 6x-5y & = & 7 \\ 8x-3y & = & -9 \end{array} $$

A.

  1. Express $x+3y=9$ in terms of $x$ as $x=9-3y$
  2. Substitute the expression from step 1 into $2x-y=4$ and obtain $2(9-3x)-y=4$.
  3. Solve the single variable equation: $$ \begin{array}{rcl} 2(9-3y)-y & = & 4 \\ 18-6y-y & = & 4 \\ 18-7y & = & 4 \\ -7y & = & 4-18 \\ -7y & = & -14 \\ y & = & \frac{-14}{-7} \\ y & = & 2 \end{array} $$
  4. Substitute the value from step 3 ($y=2$) into one of the original equations and saolve for the second variable: $$ \begin{array}{rcl} 2x-y & = & 4 \\ 2x-4 & = & 4 \\ 2x & = & 6 \\ x & = & 3 \end{array} $$ The solution is $(2,3)$
  5. Verify the solution: $$ \begin{array}{rcl} x+3y & = & 9 \\ 3+3 \times 2 & = & 9 \\ 3+6 & = & 9 \\ 9 & = & 9\\ \mbox{LHS} & = & \mbox{RHS} \\ & & \\ 2x-y & = & 4 \\ 2 \times 3 - 2 & = & 4 \\ 6 - 2 & = & 4 \\ 4 & = & 4 \\ \mbox{LHS} & = & \mbox{RHS} \end{array} $$

B.

Rewrite the system in slope y-intercept form. $$ \begin{array}{rcl} y & = & -x+7 \\ y & = & -\frac{\displaystyle 3}{\displaystyle 4}x+6 \end{array} $$

Solution: $(4,3)$. Verify by substituting solution into the original system and evaluate LHS and RHS. $$ \begin{array}{rcl} x+y & = & 7 \\ 4+5 & = & 7 \\ 7 & = & 7 \\ \mbox{LHS} & = & \mbox{RHS} \\ & & \\ 3x+4y & = & 24 \\ 3 \times 4+4 \times 3 & = & 24 \\ 24 & = & 24 \\ \mbox{LHS} & = & \mbox{RHS} \end{array} $$
