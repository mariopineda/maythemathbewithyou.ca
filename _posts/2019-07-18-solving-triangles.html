  1. Draw $\triangle ABC$ with $\angle B=27^\circ$, $a=10 \mbox{ cm}$, $\angle A=90^\circ$.
  2. Determine length of $c$ to the nearest tenth.
  3. Using a primary trionometric ratio, determine $\angle C$ to the nearest whole degree.
  4. Without using a trigonometric ratio determine the length of $b$ to the nearest tenth.

A.

B.

$$ \begin{array}{rcl} \cos{27^\circ} & = & \frac{\displaystyle c}{\displaystyle 10} \\ c & = & 10 \times \cos{27^\circ}\\ c & = & 8.9100... \approx 8.9 \mbox{ cm} \end{array} $$

C.

$$ \begin{array}{rcl} \sin{\angle C} & = & \frac{\displaystyle 8.9...}{\displaystyle 10} \\ \angle C & = & \sin^{-1}{\left( )\frac{\displaystyle 8.9100...}{\displaystyle 10} \right)} \\ \angle C & = & 63^\circ \end{array} $$

D.

$$ \begin{array}{rcl} b^2 + 8.9...^2 & = & 10^2 \\ b^2 & = & 10^2-8.9...^2 \\ b & = & \sqrt{10^2-8.9...^2} \\ b & = & 4.5399... \approx 4.5 \mbox{ cm} \end{array} $$
